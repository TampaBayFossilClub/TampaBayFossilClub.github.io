President's Message

FossilFest in the works

By the time you read this, it will be darned near game time. There are a lot people who have been working hard to make FossilFest 2022 happen. Unless you’re new to TBFC, you already know that COVID shut the show down for 2020 and 2021. We lost tons of wasted man-hours preparing for those shows that didn’t happen. We lost $$$ too. Most of the funds we depend on to financially support TBFC comes from FossilFest. We’ve been living from our savings for two years now. I can tell you all that we are excited, but a little gun-shy at this point. Here’s what has to happen.

As I write this, we are about 50 volunteers short for running the show. What that means is, we may not be able to run every feature of the show that we have in the past. Our plan is to march forward with what we have. If at the opening of the show we only have enough volunteers to run the gate and support the vendors, that’s all we will do. If we get enough to add activities like Kids Mine, Star Search, and ID tables, we’ll do that. Everybody has whispered in my ear for a year or more that we need to get TBFC back to normal. Well folks, it’s time to walk the walk. We need volunteers to pull this off.

We have enough vendors at this point (we have room for more though). That was a problem last year and the main reason we can- celled. Vendors just weren’t ready to travel yet. This part of the equation is taken care of. The last part... We need customers to show up to FossilFest. In all honesty, we need about a thousand paying adults to come through the door. If we can get patrons to show up, we just might pull this off without taking it in the shorts.

So, please volunteer if you’re able. If you’re just a fossil fan and not ready to volunteer quite yet, please make plans to show up to the show. Please tell any of your friends that may have similar interests to visit FossilFest too. Share FossilFest on your social media. We’ve all been shut up in a box for two years. This is the biggest paleo event in the south, so let’s get it done.

I THANK YOU ALL!

Peace River Adventure

I’m sorry to tell you all that we won’t be holding the Adventure this year. At least not as you’ve known it. At this point in time, all hands are on deck to support FossilFest, and as you’ve read above, we‘re hoping we can make that a success. I just can’t ask my Board to invest the time and resources involved to run the weekend camping/hunting event without knowing if people will even show up. FossilFest should be our litmus test to see what the future holds. If FossilFest comes off without a hitch, we’ll start making plans to get TBFC back to normal. But don’t walk out on me just yet...

Even though we won’t hold the adventure, we are going to have some extra field trips in lieu of the event. We will have a Peace River weekend hunt at Thousand Trails, a one day walk in hunt and a canoe trip, all on the same weekend. Check out the Field Trip page in this edition of the Chronicles for more info. There will be plenty of opportunity to hunt as a group, we just won’t be doing the din- ners, shirts, and auction, etc.

Cross your fingers this all works out. Be safe out there.

Please keep a watch out for any changes that COVID could cause with our scheduled events.

Read the Chronicles each month (Members Only), check the calendar here on the TBFC website, and follow us on Facebook. Also, please be very cautious while venturing out into the rivers and creeks. There’s nothing in those rivers worth dying for.

Good Hunting!

Mike Searle

Announcements

March 05, 2022
Dr. Bruce MacFadden, Florida Museum of Natural History
Fossils in the Schools

7PM IN-PERSON CLUB MEETING at the USF Behavioral Science Room #103
There will NOT be a livestream.

We will also *try* to be Live on YouTube but the live stream quality is not guarnteed and the stream might not even work! But we will try! (Note: some speakers may not want to be filmed). At the very least we will try to get a recording uploaded after the meeting. On the UF Faculty since 1977, Dr. MacFadden is currently a distinguished professor at the Florida Museum of Natural History (FMNH) at the University of Florida in Gainesville. He received his bachelor’s degree from Cornell University and his Ph.D. in geology from Columbia University, with a specialization in paleontology.

Dr. Bruce has been a mainstay in Florida paleontology going on 50 years now. He has been involved with most of the initiatives prompting Florida paleontology from the fieldwork, all the way over to the financing side. Many of the programs, exhibits, and discoveries in Florida are due to Dr. Bruce’s continued good work.

He is the author of 200 peer-reviewed scientific articles and 1992 book on Fossil Horses (Cambridge) and has been principal investigator on more than 50 external grants, mostly from the National Science Foundation totaling nearly $40 million. In 2009-2010 he was a Program Officer in the Education Directorate at NSF and in 2015- 2016 was a Visiting Scientist at the Santa Cruz County Office of Education in California.

Many fossil clubs and societies communicate knowledge and enthusiasm about fossils for the benefit of K-12 schools. Over the past decade the Florida Museum has undertaken several innovative projects that bring fossils and scientists into Florida schools for teaching and learning. Dr. Bruce will be discussing these initiatives, including the "Scientist in Every Florida School" program. This will include a video, PowerPoint presentation, and discussion. And as always, Dr. Bruce will bring updates from the museum and collections. I hope to see all of you at the March meeting.
