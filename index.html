Announcements

March 7, 2020
Dr. Sarah Sheffield
Paleozoic Echinoderms

Dr. Sheffield comes to us from right here at the University of South Florida. Her research interests focus on understanding the evolutionary history of life by using Paleozoic echinoderms as a model group.

Her current projects include: (1) understanding the homologous elements of echinoderm skeletons to better understand evolutionary relationships; (2) determining the phylogenetic relationships of major groups of echinoderms; (3) understanding rates of character evolution of certain morphological traits; and (4) finding new localities of echinoderm fossils in under sampled geographic areas in order to better understand the diversity of life in the Paleozoic.

Dr. Sheffields presentation focuses on the echinoderms, the group that includes sea stars, which have had a unique evolutionary history, one that has been difficult to unravel due to many factors. Low global sampling and complicated body plating patterns, to name a few. This lecture explores the evolutionary history of blastozoan echinoderms, an extinct and diverse group that lived in the Paleozoic Era hundreds of millions of years ago, and how their evolution can inform us of larger global patterns through time.
