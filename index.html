President's Message

2022-2023 Season Begins!

Welcome back fossil hunters, it’s that time of year again! The kids are back to school, the dog days of summer will hopefully come to an end soon, and it’s time to wake the Tampa Bay Fossil Club from its summer hibernation! As usual, you’re TBFC Officers and Directors have already been hard at work preparing for you yet another great season of interesting meetings, informative lectures, and as many field trip adventures that we can! Are you ready TBFC?

TBFC’s return from our summer break is always an exciting time for us all. The University of South Florida has confirmed our schedule for the season and it seems there will be no COVID restrictions placed on our meetings. All normal activities are being prepared for our 2022-2023 season. I want to tell you that we appreciate all of your support for TBFC during these past years with COVID. Attendance at the meetings was a little low this last season, but most of you kept your membership up to date!

TBFC’s Facebook page continues to do great with lots of activity and over 3,000 members. Is it too early to say that TBFC survived COVID? It sure seems that we did, and we will continue to survive any obstacles the Quaternary period has to throw at us. We did have a Jurassic outbreak of COVID with TBFC volunteers who set up the club’s wares at the Jurassic World Dominion premier. Eight of us showed up to volunteer for the event, 4 of us went home with COIVD. We will keep making TBFC work as these obstacles persist. In that vein, here we go again...

Please keep a watch out for any changes that COVID could cause with our scheduled events.

Read the Chronicles each month (Members Only), check the calendar here on the TBFC website, and follow us on Facebook. Also, please be very cautious while venturing out into the rivers and creeks. There’s nothing in those rivers worth dying for.

Denise Neumann
1964 – 2022
Former TBFC Editor, Director

Before I close here, I have to tell you the sad news that TBFC’s former editor, Denise Neumann, passed away on June 17. Denise took over our editor duties back in 2007 and did a great job for 8 years until 2015. When Denise vol- unteered to be the editor, TBFC was in a pickle. We had no one who knew how to do the editor job and our first deadline of the season was quickly approaching. Denise stepped forward and said she’d figure it out, and she did. Only after health issues began to slow her down did she give up the position. Not only did she do a great job on the Chronicles, I have to credit Denise with moving the Fossil Chronicles into the digital age. She made it very easy for me when I took over the duties.

On a personal note, Denise was married to my cousin, so she’d always been family to me. My cousin Altren (Al) couldn’t care less about fossils but he was always there to support Denise in her TBFC and paleo interests. They volunteered for every TBFC event and put in many long hours at FossilFest. We traveled to Nebraska together, went on SCUBA trips in search of fossils, and sometimes just drove around in the car looking for gravel piles at construction sites. Denise was only 57 and had been afflicted with some of the worst health and medical luck you can imagine. The last few years were tough for Denise and her family.

We love you Denise and will always miss you. Denise leaves behind her beautiful spirit, her husband Altren (Al), son Altren (IV) (wife Carissa), daughter Danielle (husband Rob) and two beautiful grandchildren. Rest in peace.

I look forward to seeing all of you at the meetings this season.

Good Hunting,

Mike Searle

Tampa Bay Fossil Chronicles

Hopefully you’ve noticed that TBFC has stepped up its game with the newsletter each month. In addition to all of our regular contributors, we’ve added two new recurring articles this season. “In Touch with Inverts” by Rob Carlson was added in September. Recently added, “Paleo Analysis” by Steve Vicari. Each month Steve will be discussing interesting prehistoric fauna and how they relate to extant species.

Please check out all of the articles in each issue. Only TBFC members receive the Chronicles, we don’t post it online or on Facebook.
