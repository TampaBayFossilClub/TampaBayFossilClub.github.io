President's Message

FossilFest Success!

Well, we did it. Finally. It had been three years since we opened the doors to FossilFest pre-COVID back in 2019. Like riding a bike though, we all knew what to do and with little problems, the show went off flawlessly. I couldn’t be more pleased with the results and the people who made it happen.

The return of FossilFest brought in a great crowd. Especially on Sunday! The question I get as soon as FossilFest is wrapped up is, “How many?” Everyone wants to know how many patrons we hosted for the show. Initial estimates show we brought in about 851 adults on Saturday and 518 on Sunday. That just might be a new record for a Sunday at FossilFest.

There are three major goals each year for FossilFest; earn funds to operate TBFC through the year, teach the public about Florida’s prehistory, and reach out to new members. We accomplished all of that! It looks like we had about 30 new families sign up as members with TBFC at FossilFest. That in itself is a great success! If you’re one of those new members that joined us, I welcome you to the Tampa Bay Fossil Club!

Everyone worked very hard, some for three hours, some for three days (some for weeks leading up to the show). I hate to thank individual volunteers by name in this writing, but I have to mention a few people here. TBFC Directors David Faucher and Joe Branin spent the night on the FossilFest floor both Friday and Saturday night to stand sentry for the show. And both worked the show all weekend. All in all, they spent over 50 straight hours at FossilFest.

Steve Vicari and Nancy Jacobs have to get the award for furthest traveled. They drove over a thousand miles (each way) from Rusk, Texas to be with us and volunteer at FossilFest. Both started working early in the week helping with the load up and set at Fox 13.

I have to thank my wife Seina for working on the volunteer assignments for months ahead of the show, and many of the other details at Fossilfest that just seem to “happen” because of her. Every year for as long as I can remember Barbara Fite has led the negotiations with the Fairgrounds and scheduled the vendors. She also handles many other problems as they occur. Jerry Zolg spends many hours leading up to the show arranging, and rearranging the floor plan to fit our needs, and sometimes the last-minute changes that have to happen. Our FossilFest trailer is big and weighs in excess of 6 tons. We need a huge truck to make that move and for many years Mike and Jessica Burnett have driven his “monster truck” from Bradenton, Florida to pick up the trailer in Zephyrhills where it is stored on the property of TBFC members Darren and Nicole Watts. By the time they drive from Bradenton to ZHills, hooks up, drives back to the fairgrounds, and then back home, it’s an eight-hour day on the road. The Burnett’s do this on Friday, in morning traffic, and then again on Sunday. Hidden in all of the excitement of FossilFest is Bill Faucher collecting money, making change, and getting money safely to the bank. Dr. Bob Sinibaldi assisted me with television appearances and also did two presentations each day at FossilFest. Leslie Branin and her husband Mike Davis hauled a metric ton of books to the show. Fred Hendershot and Joe Dumont spent the entire week- end with kids at the Touch Table and Kid’s Mine. We didn’t have a standalone Kid’s Mine manager this year, the duties were split between about a dozen volunteers. It takes a whole lot of patience and dedication to educate and to deal with all of the excitement in the kid’s area. A special thank you to those volunteers who sat for hours at the Kid’s Mine ID table. Marlin Swikert managed the Silent Auction and along with John and Cheryl Jacobs and other volun- teers, killed it (that’s a good thing the kids tell me). And that’s just some of the many big things that get done at FossilFest.

I have to thank all of TBFC’s Officers, Directors, and ALL of the members who volunteered to make FossilFest the success that it was. We certainly couldn’t have done it without you. If you were one of the 120 plus volunteers during the show, thank you. From the admissions, to the silent auction, donations, club sales, kids mine, set up, break down, security, and everything in-between, I can’t thank you all enough.

I get lots of praises and slaps on the back during FossilFest. I always tell everyone what a great support staff that I, and FossilFest, have. FossilFest 2022 was a huge success, and that’s because of all of you.

So, I have to ask... Are you still tired from FossilFest? Sorry, no time to sit down; TBFC’s season isn’t over yet, we’re just getting started!

Peace River Adventure (Sort of)

Even though there won’t be a full blown Peace River Adventure this year, there will be field trips in lieu of the event. There will be a weekend hunt at Thousand Trails (April 30 – May 1) a one day walk in hunt and canoe trip at Wauchula (April 30). So, there will be plenty of opportunity to hunt as a group, we just won’t be doing the dinners, shirts, and auction, etc. This will also present the opportunity for our new members fresh out of FossilFest to join in on a fossil hunt. With the success of Fos- silFest last month, I certainly think we’ll be back to a normal Peace River Adventure in 2023.

Please keep a watch out for any changes that COVID could cause with our scheduled events.

Read the Chronicles each month (Members Only), check the calendar here on the TBFC website, and follow us on Facebook. Also, please be very cautious while venturing out into the rivers and creeks. There’s nothing in those rivers worth dying for.

In closing, I want to again say thank you to everyone who helped out this past month. Thank you for making FossilFest, and everything else about TBFC, so great.

I look forward to seeing all of the new faces at the April meeting.

Good Hunting,

Mike Searle

Announcements

April 02, 2022
Doug Woodring, Sharkless Tooth Enterprises
The Sharkless Tooth

7PM IN-PERSON CLUB MEETING at the USF Behavioral Science Room #103
There will NOT be a livestream but the meeting will be recorded.

The recording will be uploaded to our YouTube a day or so after the meeting. (Note: some speakers may not want to be filmed).

Growing up in the Poconos of Pennsylvania in the 1970’s and 80’s Doug got hooked on fossils at an early age when he was given a trilobite fossil and enjoyed school field trips to collect fern fossils from the local shale. He read all the books he could on fossils and dinosaurs and even dreamed of one day being a paleontologist.

Doug pursued an Astronomy degree at Penn State University from 1981-1985, but switched to General Science in order to graduate on time, and earn a commission in the U.S. Navy. In 1997 Doug began SCUBA diving and later retired from the navy in 2005. Having settled in the Tampa area with his wife, Cathi, he was still not shark tooth diving, yet. But in 2012 Doug made his first Venice shark tooth dive. His wife Cathi was certified in 2013 and both are hooked on fossil shark teeth. So much so, they purchased a condo on the island of Venice, Florida near Golden Beach to dive in pursuit of the coveted megalodon teeth and various Ice Age fossils. Doug is also a PADI instructor and Master SCUBA Diver.

Doug’s presentation will not only cover his experiences diving for shark teeth, but also outline how natural forces shaped and ulti- mately created fossil-rich Florida. Doug and I both look forward to seeing all of you at the April meeting.

Tampa Bay Fossil Chronicles

In addition to all of our regular contributors, we’ve added two new recurring articles this season. “In Touch with Inverts” by Rob Carlson was added in September. Please check out all of the articles in each issue. Only TBFC members receive the Chronicles, we don’t post it online or on Facebook.
