President's Message

2021-2022 Season is almost at an End!

It feels like just yesterday that we were busy making plans to start TBFC’s season, and just like that, it’s nearly over. We had hoped to get things back a little closer to normal, and I think we succeeded. FossilFest came off without a hitch (thanks to all of you). We have field trips up and running and have had a complete season of in-person meetings. We are beginning to wrap things up, but there are still plenty of events left before TBFC takes a much deserved summer hiatus. If you’re new to TBFC, you may not know that we shut down for the summer. TBFC will host our end of the season dinner on June 4, but there will be no regular monthly meetings held in June, July, or August. That doesn’t mean we’re finished though. Be sure to check the calendar and field trip pages as there are still a few TBFC events remaining.

June 4 - TBFC’s End of the Season Dinner

Don’t let the “banquet” thing impress you too much. It’s just a fancy word for TBFC’s annual, end of the season, informal dinner, and get together (with a scholarship presentation, fossil auction, and some door prizes thrown in for good measure). It doesn’t matter if you’ve been a member of TBFC for ten years or ten minutes, everyone’s invited. The dinner is a great opportunity for fossil friends old and new to get together for a final evening of fun and fossil tales as we wrap up the 2021-2022 season. And there’s always a bunch of great fossils and “paleo” related items in the auction (if you have anything you’d like to donate, just bring it along to the dinner). This is a great event for the entire family and cheap too. We’ll cater in spaghetti, pizza, wings, salads, dessert and drinks. The cost is just $7 per person which makes for an inexpensive family outing.

TBFC’s End of the Season Banquet will be held at 6PM on June 4 at the:

American Postal Workers Union Hall,
4409 West Alva Street,
Tampa, Florida 33614

This location is about a mile from Raymond James Stadium.

RSVP is not required, just show up and pay at the door. Don’t miss it! This really, really, is the last event of the season, whew!

Please keep a watch out for any changes that COVID could cause with our scheduled events.

Read the Chronicles each month (Members Only), check the calendar here on the TBFC website, and follow us on Facebook. Also, please be very cautious while venturing out into the rivers and creeks. There’s nothing in those rivers worth dying for.

Announcements

May 07, 2022
Dr. Joshua Slattery, Marine Science, Eckerd College
Ecological Role of Ammonites

7PM IN-PERSON CLUB MEETING at the USF Behavioral Science Room #103
There will NOT be a livestream but the meeting will be recorded.

The recording will be uploaded to our YouTube a day or so after the meeting. (Note: some speakers may not want to be filmed).

Dr. Joshua is also a long time member of TBFC and participates regularly in club meetings and events. He also serves officially as a TBFC Paleo Advisor. Raised in Wyoming, Dr. Joshua took special interest in marine inverts at an early age. If you talk to Dr. Joshua, you’ll quickly realize that his favorite critter, by far, is the ammonite.

Ammonite cephalopods are among the most diverse, abundant and iconic invertebrates of the Mesozoic. They have been extensively studied by scientist and are sought after by collectors for their beauty as well as diversity. Due to their rapid evolutionary rates,ammonite species typically exhibit short temporal ranges, making them useful index fossils for biostratigraphy and correlation of strata. Yet de- spite extensive knowledge of their biostratigraphy and biogeogra- phy, relatively little is known about the ecology of this important clade. The Goal of Dr. Joshua’s will be to examine what ecological role ammonites played in the Late Cretaceous marine ecosystems of North America. In addition the presentation will examine the impact that their mass extinction had on marine ecosystems and how their loss opened ecological niches that possibly resulted in the Cenozoic diversification of bony fish.

Don’t miss Dr. Joshua at the May 7 meeting!
