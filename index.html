President's Message

TBFC – 2021-2022 Season

One year ago today I was writing here with hope that TBFC would dig out of the COVID lockdown. I was wrong. Despite online meetings and a poorly attended outdoor swap meet, TBFC did not start 2021 off well. FossilFest was canceled for the second year in a row. The Peace River Adventure as well. There just weren’t enough people willing to volunteer or set up as vendors at the show. We transferred the hopes of a more normal TBFC to the 2021-2022 season.

We began in person meetings in September and now look forward to holding a successful FossilFest 2022.

If you are going to volunteer for FossilFest, please do it early. If you plan to be a vendor at the show, please sign up for your tables as soon as you can. TBFC will do everything in our power to make this work, but WE NEED YOU!

Field Trips

We have two NEW field trips in 2022. Fred will be hosting a fossil hunt at Caspersen Beach and Marlin has finally got us back into CEMEX.
Check out the Field Trips page for more info.

Join our Facebook page to connect with other fossil hunters. Organize your own group hunt or canoe trip. Meet at a boat ramp and walk in. I promise you that abundant fossils await, you just have to get out there and find them.

Be safe, social distance, and take care of each other out there.

Please keep a watch out for any changes that COVID could cause with our scheduled events.

Read the Chronicles each month (Members Only), check the calendar here on the TBFC website, and follow us on Facebook. Also, please be very cautious while venturing out into the rivers and creeks. There’s nothing in those rivers worth dying for.

Come January we will need to hit the ground running. Despite continuing challenges with COVID, FossilFest 2022 is a GO! The Peace River Adventure is a GO! I say both with enthusiasm... unless COVID shuts down the world again.

Good Hunting!

Mike Searle

Announcements

Jan. 08, 2022 (Second Saturday!)
Ice Age Florida in Story & Art with Dr. Bob Sinibaldi and Artist Hermann Trappman

7PM IN-PERSON CLUB MEETING at the USF Behavioral Science Room #103
There will NOT be a livestream.

TBFC is excited to host the book release event for Ice Age Florida, In Story and Art. Dr. Bob Sinibaldi and noted artist Hermann Trappman collaborated on the perfect blend of story, and art, for a new look at Florida’s Ice Age. Dr. Bob’s presentation will focus on aspects of the book and the multiyear long effort to get the book in print.

In a day and age when fewer and fewer books are being published, the struggle to get this type of book published may have been as arduous as writing and illustrating the book. Join us as we celebrate the release of yet another great paleo literary work by Dr. Bob and artist Hermann Trappman.

Dr. Bob will have copies of the book available at the meeting at a significant price reduction.

Hope to see you all there in person!

Ice Age Florida, In Story and Art. Sinibaldi and Trappmenn, 2021. Dorrance Publishing Co., Pittsburgh, PA. 300 Pages, ISBN: 978-1- 6480-4356-7, Retail price: $70, eBook $10.

Tampa Bay Fossil Chronicles

Hopefully you’ve noticed that TBFC has stepped up its game with the newsletter each month. In addition to all of our regular contributors, we’ve added two new recurring articles this season. “In Touch with Inverts” by Rob Carlson was added in September. Recently added, “Paleo Analysis” by Steve Vicari. Each month Steve will be discussing interesting prehistoric fauna and how they relate to extant species.

Please check out all of the articles in each issue. Only TBFC members receive the Chronicles, we don’t post it online or on Facebook.
