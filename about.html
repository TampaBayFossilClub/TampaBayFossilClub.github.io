About Us

We hope you will feel at home here as you browse each section. Please don't hesitate to contact us with questions or comments. And if you're not already a member, what are you waiting for?!

The Tampa Bay Fossil Club is a family oriented organization focusing on paleontology, archaeology, and everything prehistoric. Meetings are held monthly at the University of South Floridaâ€™s Tampa campus and features prominent speakers from many areas of science.

The Tampa Bay Fossil Club awards annual scholarships to students studying in the field of paleontology and geology. The 30+ year-old club hosts numerous field trips, campouts, gatherings, and adventures throughout its September through May seasons.

Come Join Us!

Florida emerged from the warm Atlantic Ocean about 30 million years ago, 35 million years after dinosaurs became extinct. For that reason, no one has ever found any dinosaur bones in Florida. However, ancient mammals and sharks thrived here, including such unusual creatures as two-story tall giant ground sloths, Volkswagen Beetle-size armadillos, tiny three-toed horses, humpless camels,hornless rhinos, fierce saber-toothed cats, massive mammoths and sharks as large as a Greyhound bus.

Florida's first people were here to witness--if not participate in--the demise of many of these animals some 10,000 to 15,000 years ago.
